BrandGEO Publishes Its First AI Visibility Index Report

BrandGEO Editorial Team July 14, 2026 2 min read

SANTA CRUZ DE TENERIFE, SPAIN. BrandGEO has published Issue #1 of the AI Visibility Index, a new monthly report built entirely from the company's own collection data rather than survey estimates or vendor claims. The first issue draws on 419 real AI responses collected between July 6 and July 13, 2026, across 12 accounts and 5 AI engines, anchored by a 7-city research program covering London, Berlin, Madrid, New York, Paris, Rome, and Dublin.

The report's central finding is a split that held across every city tested: commercial categories with a small number of well-documented, frequently-reviewed products, such as CRM software, fintech banking apps, and accounting tools, produce near-unanimous agreement among AI engines on which brand to recommend. Categories built on a long tail of small local firms, including employment law and independent financial advice, show no consistent agreement at all. A separate finding from the Paris data shows that the language a question is asked in can change which entire class of competitor an AI engine recommends, not just which specific brand comes out on top.

BrandGEO is publishing the first issue with its limitations stated directly rather than waiting for a larger dataset. The underlying city research reflects a single collection pass per city rather than a longitudinal trend, and a known collection error affected ChatGPT across every prompt in this run. Real, paying-client collection data only began accumulating on a daily basis as of July 13, 2026, which the company says will allow future issues to show genuine month-over-month movement rather than single-city snapshots.

"We had two choices: wait until the dataset was bigger and call it a launch, or publish what we actually have and say exactly what it does and doesn't prove yet," said a BrandGEO spokesperson. "We picked the second one. A caveated real number is worth more than a polished guess, and that's the standard we're holding every future issue to."
