BrandGEO Research™ BG-004 Case Study Gemini AI Visibility

Ranked #1 on Three AI Engines. Completely Absent on Gemini.

We ran the same 20 real customer questions through 5 AI engines for a market-leading catering brand. The results expose exactly why "ranking well" and "being AI Visible" are not the same thing.

The audit that exposed the gap

We recently ran an AI Visibility audit for a market-leading catering company — the kind of brand that shows up on page one of Google without trying very hard. The test was simple: 20 real questions a prospective customer might actually ask, run identically across 5 major AI engines.

The goal wasn't to prove a point. It was to find out, objectively, where this brand actually stands once you stop assuming that a strong Google presence means a strong AI presence everywhere else.

#1 on three platforms — and confident about it

On ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot, the brand came back as the top recommendation, consistently, across the majority of the 20 questions. On Claude, it was mentioned — present in the conversation, if not always the first name out.

By any normal read of "how are we doing," this looks like a dominant market position. Three engines naming you first, unprompted, for real customer questions, is a strong result by any standard.

Completely absent on Gemini

On Gemini, the same brand, answering the same 20 questions, appeared zero times.

That's the counterintuitive part. As we noted in BG-001, Gemini has deep integration with Google's own web index and structured data — it's the engine you'd expect to most closely mirror strong organic SEO performance. A brand with page-one Google rankings should, in theory, have an advantage here.

In practice, Gemini doesn't simply echo Google's organic rankings back as recommendations. It synthesizes an answer from the entity and citation signals it can verify — and if those signals aren't structured the way Gemini's synthesis process expects (the exact "sources & signals" stage we broke down in BG-003), a brand can rank #1 organically and still not exist in the generated answer at all.

The uncomfortable takeaway

You cannot infer your AI Visibility on one engine from your performance on another — not even from Google itself. Each AI engine builds its own independent picture of your brand, and a gap on any single one of them stays completely invisible until someone actually checks.

Why "ranking well" isn't the same as being visible everywhere

This is the pattern we see over and over: brands assume AI Visibility is a single score, when it's actually five or seven separate scores that don't move together. Strength on one engine tells you almost nothing about your exposure on the others.

For a catering brand, a gap like this on Gemini isn't abstract — it's every prospective client who asks Google's AI for a recommendation and gets a competitor's name instead, silently, with no way for the brand to know it's happening.

What this means for your brand

If you've never tested your brand engine by engine, you don't actually know your real exposure. You might be exactly like this catering brand: dominant everywhere you've checked, and quietly invisible somewhere you haven't.

The only way to find out is to ask the same real questions across every engine your customers actually use — and to keep asking, since these results shift as AI engines update their models and their sources.
