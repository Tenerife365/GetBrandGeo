AI Visibility in Paris: When Language Changes Which Brands Get Recommended

Like Berlin and Madrid, Paris was tested bilingually — 4 categories, each asked once in French and once in English, across four AI engines. One category produced the cleanest, most unanimous consensus found anywhere in this research program: a single online bank, named first by every engine, in both languages, every time.

A different category showed something this program hadn't seen before: the language of the question didn't just reorder the same names — it changed the entire type of competitor the AI considered.

When language changes the category, not just the ranking

For wealth management advisors, French-language answers consistently named independent, boutique French patrimoine firms — Cheval Blanc Patrimoine appeared in 3 of 4 French-language responses and in zero English-language ones. English-language answers instead converged on major international private banks like Société Générale Private Banking and BNP Paribas Wealth Management. This isn't the same brands reordered by language — it's a genuinely different competitive set, depending only on which language the question was asked in. A boutique firm that only publishes in French is structurally invisible to English-language AI searchers in this category, not just lower-ranked.

One bank, every engine, every language

The online-banking prompt produced the cleanest single-brand result found anywhere in this research program to date. Qonto was named the #1 recommendation by every engine that returned a structured result — Claude, Gemini, Meta AI, and Perplexity — in both French and English, with no exceptions. Shine was the consistent #2 pick across the same responses. For a category where an AI recommendation converts directly into a new business-banking signup, that's about as strong an AI Visibility position as a brand can hold.

A data-quality note, not a finding

ChatGPT's collection failed on every Paris prompt this run (a technical error, `status: error`) — it isn't included in the tables above. Meta AI is also missing one full response (the English fine-dining prompt) — a collection gap, not a zero-competitor answer. Separately, Gemini and Meta both returned blank competitor lists on the English online-banking prompt despite Meta's own prose explicitly naming Qonto — an extraction miss specific to that response, not a real absence of an opinion.

What this means if your business is in Paris

  1. 1

    If your category shows a language-driven category shift, publish in both languages deliberately

    Wealth management shows that being visible in French doesn't guarantee any visibility in English, because AI engines may be drawing from an entirely different pool of considered brands per language. Don't assume one language's presence covers the other.

  2. 2

    If your category is unanimous, find out if you're the name it gives

    The online-banking category shows what total AI convergence looks like — one name, every engine, every language. A challenger brand absent from that answer has a specific, large gap to close, not a vague one.

  3. 3

    Fragmented categories are the easiest door in

    Fine dining for business dinners shows no strong cross-engine leader. That's the category type where a newer or smaller establishment has the best real chance of becoming an AI-recommended name.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, in each language, this week. As this data shows, both language coverage and extraction quality can shift between runs — a check from months ago won't tell you what's true now.

● Common Questions

Which Paris brand had the strongest AI consensus?

Qonto, for online business banking. It was named the #1 recommendation by every AI engine that returned a structured result, in both French and English — a complete, unanimous sweep, one of the strongest single-brand results found anywhere in this research program.

Does asking in French vs. English just reorder the same brands, or something bigger?

For wealth management specifically, it's bigger. French-language answers consistently surfaced independent, boutique French patrimoine firms, while English-language answers converged on major international private banks. The entire type of competitor considered shifted by language, not just the ranking of the same names.

Why is ChatGPT missing from this data?

ChatGPT's collection failed on every Paris prompt this run — a technical error, not evidence ChatGPT has zero visibility for Paris brands. This page reflects the engines that returned usable results: Claude, Gemini, Meta AI, and Perplexity. A future re-run will include ChatGPT once the collection succeeds.
