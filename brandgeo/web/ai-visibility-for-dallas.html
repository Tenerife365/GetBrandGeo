AI Visibility in Dallas: Real Estate Agents Are Wide Open

We ran 6 real Dallas-Fort Worth categories (personal injury law, real estate, property management, immigration law, corporate relocation, and home building) through the same AI Visibility pipeline BrandGEO runs for paying clients, across five AI engines. The headline: Hamilton Wingo and Mullen & Mullen were named #1 and #2, in that exact order, by every engine that returned a full ranking, while real estate agents produced zero cross-engine overlap at all, the most fragmented single category measured in this research program.

Property management and corporate relocation both showed solid mid-tier consensus. Home builders and real estate agents stayed wide open, no incumbent has locked either category down.

A clean, identical rank order across three engines

Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity all name Hamilton Wingo, LLP the #1 personal injury firm in Dallas, and all three name Mullen & Mullen Law Firm #2, the exact same order, independently. That's a stronger signal than presence alone: three separate models converged on not just who, but in what sequence.

Real estate agents are the most fragmented category measured anywhere in this program

Every one of the 5 engines named a completely different set of agents and brokerages for "best real estate agents for buying a home in Dallas-Fort Worth," ChatGPT referenced a matching tool, Claude led with an individual agent, Gemini and Google AI Mode both named different brokerages, and Perplexity named yet another team entirely. Zero names repeat across any two engines, a more extreme fragmentation than the same category showed in New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, or Houston.

A data-quality note, not a finding

All 5 engines returned usable data on every prompt this run, no collection errors. Two flagged as noise: "HomeLight" and "Dave Ramsey RamseyTrusted," both agent-matching/referral services, not real estate agents themselves. Separately, Claude and Gemini both named luxury real estate brokerages in response to a "corporate relocation consultants" prompt, blending two different service categories rather than sticking to moving-logistics firms, a genuine engine-behavior quirk, not a data error. This run also used Google AI Mode in place of the now-retired Meta AI engine used in earlier city pages.

    Engines blended real estate brokerages into "corporate relocation" answers here. If your business sits at the boundary between two service categories, verify which one AI engines actually associate you with.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, this week. Engine behavior and extraction quality both shift between runs. A one-time check tells you less than an ongoing one.
