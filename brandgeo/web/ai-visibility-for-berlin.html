AI Visibility in Berlin: German vs. English Answers from the Same AI Engines

Berlin is a bilingual market by nature — German-speaking locals, an English-speaking international startup scene — so it's the one city in this research program we asked in both languages. We ran 4 categories as paired German/English prompts through BrandGEO's own collection pipeline against four AI engines, then compared not just which brands came up, but whether the same engine gave a different answer depending on the language it was asked in.

The result: some brands cut through in both languages. Two engines gave detailed answers in German and returned nothing at all for the identical question in English. That's not a translation issue — it's a real, measurable gap in how AI engines treat a market depending on the language you approach it in.

The language gap, in practice

For the HR-software question, Gemini and Meta AI both gave rich, multi-brand answers in German — but when asked the identical question in English, both returned nothing usable at all. Personio, Germany's dominant HR platform, was the one brand strong enough to come through in both languages, though via different engines each time (Gemini/Meta/Perplexity in German, only Perplexity in English). Most other brands' visibility depended entirely on which language the question was asked in — a real, measurable version of the multilingual AI Visibility gap BrandGEO's own research has already flagged (see BG-014, linked below).

Why some categories converge and others don't

The pattern matches what BrandGEO found running the same kind of test in London: categories with a small number of well-documented, category-defining products (Lexware for accounting, Personio for HR) get near-unanimous AI answers, because there's a clear, consistent signal for every engine to converge on. Employment law and private clinics — categories with thousands of small firms and individual practitioners, each with a thin and inconsistent online footprint — produce answers where barely any two engines agree, in either language.

A data-quality note, not a finding

ChatGPT's collection failed on every Berlin prompt this run (a technical error, `status: error`) — it isn't included in the tables above. That's not evidence ChatGPT has zero visibility for Berlin brands; it's a gap in this specific data pull that a future re-run will close. Worth knowing before drawing any conclusion about ChatGPT specifically from this page.

What this means if your business is in Berlin

  1. 1

    Check your visibility in both German and English

    This data shows an engine can answer confidently in one language and go blank in the other for the exact same question. If your business serves Berlin's international startup scene, your English-language AI visibility is not guaranteed by your German-language visibility, or vice versa.

  2. 2

    If your category is unanimous, find out if you're the name it gives

    Accounting and HR software both have engines that have already converged on a short list. A challenger brand absent from that list has a real, measurable gap to close — not a hypothetical one.

  3. 3

    If your category is fragmented, treat it as an open door

    Employment law shows zero cross-engine agreement in either language right now. That's the easiest kind of category to become visible in, because no incumbent has locked down the AI narrative yet.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, in each language, this week. AI-generated recommendations shift as engines re-crawl directories, reviews, and press coverage — and, as this page shows, language coverage itself can shift between runs.

● Common Questions

Why did you ask each question in both German and English?

Because the language a question is asked in can genuinely change what an AI engine answers — not just the wording, but which brands get named at all. Berlin is a bilingual market by nature (German-speaking locals, an English-speaking international startup scene), so it was the natural city to test this directly.

Did the answers actually change depending on language?

Yes, sometimes dramatically. For the HR-software question, Gemini and Meta AI both gave detailed, multi-brand answers in German but returned no usable answer at all when asked the identical question in English. Personio was the one brand that came through in both languages — via different engines each time — but most other brands' visibility was language-dependent.

Why is ChatGPT missing from this data?

ChatGPT's collection run failed on every Berlin prompt this cycle — a technical error, not evidence that ChatGPT has zero visibility for Berlin brands. This page reflects the 4 engines that returned usable results: Claude, Gemini, Meta AI, and Perplexity. A future re-run will include ChatGPT once the collection succeeds.
