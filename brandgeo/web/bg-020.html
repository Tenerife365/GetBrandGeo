The Bug Where Our Own Outage Looked Like Your AI Visibility Problem

An AI engine that timed out on our end, and an AI engine that genuinely has never heard of your brand, used to produce the exact same number in our pipeline: zero. One of those is our billing problem. Only the other is a real finding.

The number zero was doing two different jobs

Somewhere in the first months of running BrandGEO's own AI Visibility Score, we found a bug that was uncomfortable in a specific way: it wasn't wrong about the brand, it was wrong about us. When an AI engine call failed, quota exhausted, a timeout, an authentication error, the pipeline recorded that engine as contributing zero mentions to the brand's score. When an AI engine call succeeded and the engine genuinely had never heard of the brand, the pipeline also recorded zero mentions. Both cases collapsed into the same number, and that number then told a prospect they had a visibility problem, in some cases when the actual problem was our own outage.

That is a different category of error than the extraction and rank bugs we've written about previously. Those miscounted what an AI answer said. This one miscounted whether we ever successfully asked the question at all.

The tell was already sitting in the data

The fix didn't require new instrumentation, because the distinguishing signal already existed in how results get written. An engine that runs and simply doesn't mention the brand still writes a row, with brand_mentioned: false. An engine that fails outright, before it ever produces an answer, writes no row at all. Zero rows and a row with a false value look identical if you only check "was the brand mentioned," but they are not the same event, and conflating them was the entire bug.

enginesWithResults now filters explicitly for engines that produced at least one row, and that filtered set, not the original list of engines requested, becomes the denominator for the reach dimension of the score. An engine we asked and never heard back from is excluded from the calculation entirely, rather than counted as a mention of zero.

The concrete difference

Before the fix: ask 5 engines, 1 fails outright, 2 mention the brand. Reach = 2/5 = 40%. After the fix: the failed engine is excluded from the denominator. Reach = 2/4 = 50%. Ten points of score, on every affected audit, that had nothing to do with the brand's actual AI visibility and everything to do with whether our own API call succeeded that day.

From a binary read to four honest states

The same fix propagated into how we report per-engine status. A binary "mentions you / doesn't mention you" read was replaced with four distinct states, computed by computeEngineStates: an engine with zero rows for a client is unavailable, not "missing," because zero rows means we failed to ask, not that the engine answered and said no. Only an engine that actually produced rows and never mentioned the brand across them is scored missing. Above that, the same 60% mention-rate threshold the score's existing consistency dimension already uses, not a new cutoff invented for this fix, separates know from partial.

StateWhat actually happened
knowEngine produced rows; mentioned the brand in ≥60% of checked prompts
partialEngine produced rows; mentioned the brand in some but under 60%
missingEngine produced rows; never mentioned the brand in any of them — a real finding
unavailableEngine produced zero rows — we failed to ask, not a finding about the brand

The code comment attached to this fix is blunt about what the old behavior cost: it "told a prospect an AI engine had never heard of them when the truth was our quota ran out. Never again." That line is now a standing guardrail in the source, not just a commit message, precisely so the distinction doesn't quietly erode the next time this function gets touched.

Why this mattered more on the gap-finding side

The same underlying data also feeds computeGapsAndFlags, which surfaces a prospect's most compelling visibility gaps, prioritising cases where a named competitor was recommended instead of the brand over plain not-mentioned results. A gap sourced from an unavailable engine would have been the least honest kind of finding possible: "AI Engine X recommends a competitor instead of you" when AI Engine X was never actually asked. Excluding unavailable engines from this surface, not just from the reach percentage, was as important as the scoring fix itself.

What's next in this series

Reach is one of six dimensions folded into the composite AI Visibility Score. We'll publish the full weighting, and the reasoning behind it, in our next piece.

What this means if you're comparing AI visibility vendors

An outage-proof score isn't a nice-to-have feature, it's a baseline requirement for the number to mean what it claims to mean. Any pipeline that asks multiple AI engines per prompt has to handle partial failure somehow, and the honest way to handle it is to exclude what you couldn't measure, not to silently score it as a negative result. If a vendor's score has never moved because of their own infrastructure rather than your brand's actual visibility, ask how they know that, because the two are not always self-evidently different from the outside.
