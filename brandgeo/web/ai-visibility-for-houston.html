AI Visibility in Houston: One Hospital System Is Unanimous #1

We ran 6 real Houston categories (personal injury law, real estate, property management, immigration law, oil & gas energy law, and hospital systems) through the same AI Visibility pipeline BrandGEO runs for paying clients, across five AI engines. The headline: Houston Methodist was named the #1 hospital system by every single engine that returned a ranking, the cleanest, strongest result measured anywhere in this research program.

Oil and gas energy law showed similarly strong consensus, with two firms both hitting 4 of 5 engines. Individual real estate agents and immigration lawyers, meanwhile, stayed as fragmented as anywhere else measured.

The strongest single result measured in this research program

Houston Methodist was named the #1 hospital system by ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity, all four engines that returned a ranked list, and Google AI Mode's prose independently confirmed the same thing: "consistently ranked as the #1 hospital system in Texas." That's unanimous agreement on both presence and exact rank position, a level of consensus not seen even in Chicago's strongest categories.

Houston's AI consensus reflects its actual economy

The city's two strongest AI-visibility categories map directly onto its real economic identity: oil & gas / energy law (Vinson & Elkins and Bracewell, both 4/5, reflecting Houston's status as the Energy Capital of the World) and hospital systems (Houston Methodist, 5/5, reflecting the Texas Medical Center, the largest medical complex in the world). This is the same pattern already seen in Los Angeles (entertainment law) and Chicago (corporate law): each city's strongest AI consensus tends to land on the category tied most closely to that city's actual economic specialty.

A data-quality note, not a finding

All 5 engines returned usable data on every prompt this run, no collection errors, consistent with Los Angeles and Chicago and unlike New York (where ChatGPT failed on all 8 prompts). Structured competitor extraction was incomplete for Gemini and Google AI Mode on a few prompts; those responses were cross-checked manually against their prose. This run also used Google AI Mode in place of the now-retired Meta AI engine used in earlier city pages.

What this means if your business is in Houston

  1. 1

    If you're in healthcare or energy law, the incumbent bar is the highest measured anywhere

    Houston Methodist and the Vinson & Elkins / Bracewell duo show near-total AI agreement. A competitor absent from those answers faces the steepest documented gap in this research program.

  2. 2

    Property management keeps proving to be a winnable, high-consensus category

    Every city researched so far shows strong AI agreement on property management (3/4 in New York, 4/5 in Los Angeles, 5/5 in Chicago, 4/5 twice over in Houston). If you're in this space, getting into that consensus set is a realistic, measurable goal.

  3. 3

    Fragmented categories are still the easiest door in

    Individual real estate agents and immigration lawyers show almost no cross-engine agreement in Houston. That's where a specific agent or firm has the best real chance of becoming an AI-recommended name.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, this week. Engine behavior and extraction quality both shift between runs. A one-time check tells you less than an ongoing one.
