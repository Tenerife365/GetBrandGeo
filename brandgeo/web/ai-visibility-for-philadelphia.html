AI Visibility in Philadelphia: Two Hospital Systems Are Unanimous

We ran 6 real Philadelphia categories (personal injury law, real estate, property management, immigration law, hospital systems, and university admissions consulting) through the same AI Visibility pipeline BrandGEO runs for paying clients, across five AI engines. The headline: Penn Medicine and Jefferson Health both reached full 5-of-5 unanimous consensus for hospital systems, simultaneously, the strongest multi-brand result measured anywhere in this research program.

Property management and personal injury law both showed strong consensus too. Immigration law converged here as well, making Philadelphia the third city where that category broke its usual fragmentation pattern. Individual real estate agents, as always, stayed wide open.

The strongest multi-brand result measured in this research program

Penn Medicine is named #1 by ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity, is Claude's explicit #1 prose pick, and Google AI Mode's prose independently confirms it as the top-ranked hospital in Philadelphia and Pennsylvania. Jefferson Health is separately named by all 5 engines too. Houston's Houston Methodist was the previous strongest single-brand result at 5/5; Philadelphia now has two brands there at once.

Immigration law converged for the third time

In New York, Los Angeles, Houston, and Dallas, immigration law was consistently one of the most fragmented categories, dominated by individually-rated practitioners. Philadelphia is the third city (after Chicago and Miami) where it showed real consensus instead: Palladino, Isbell & Casazza, LLC reached 3 of 5 engines, named #1 by ChatGPT, #2 by Claude, and named first in Google AI Mode's prose. The program-wide pattern for this category is now close to an even split.

A data-quality note, not a finding

All 5 engines returned usable data on every prompt this run, no collection errors. Two directory/ratings platforms were misidentified as businesses and removed as noise: "Zillow" (property management prompt) and "Justia" / "Avvo" (immigration-law prompt). This run also used Google AI Mode in place of the now-retired Meta AI engine used in earlier city pages.

    Property management keeps proving to be a winnable, high-consensus category

    Every city researched so far shows strong AI agreement on property management. If you're in this space, getting into that consensus set is a realistic, measurable goal, not a long shot.

  3. 3

    Don't assume your category fragments just because it did somewhere else

    Immigration law fragmented in four cities but converged in three, including Philadelphia. Category behavior is genuinely city-specific: check your own market rather than assuming a pattern from elsewhere applies.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, this week. Engine behavior and extraction quality both shift between runs. A one-time check tells you less than an ongoing one.
