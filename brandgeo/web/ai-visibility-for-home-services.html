AI Visibility for Home Services & Contractors: How to Get Recommended by ChatGPT, Gemini & Perplexity

Homeowners increasingly ask an AI assistant "who should I call for an emergency leak" or "how much should a roof replacement cost" before they ever open Yelp or Google. The AI names one to three businesses directly, in seconds — and most homeowners never click through to compare further.

● How to Improve Your AI Visibility as a Home Services Business

None of this replaces local SEO — it sits on top of it. AI engines weight some of the same signals Google does, plus a few most home services businesses have never optimized for.

  1. 1

    Add LocalBusiness or HomeAndConstructionBusiness schema markup to every service-area page, not just your homepage — this is the structured data AI engines read first when trying to confirm what you do and where you operate.

  2. 2

    Build out a genuinely complete Google Business Profile — full service list, accurate categories, photos, and a steady velocity of new reviews, not just a high star rating on an old, thin profile.

  3. 3

    Keep your listings consistent across Angi, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor, Nextdoor, and the BBB. AI engines cross-reference these directories directly to verify a business is legitimate before recommending it — inconsistent name, address, or phone data across them is a real trust signal you're leaving on the table.

  4. 4

    Publish transparent pricing or cost-range pages AI can extract and quote directly — "average cost to replace a water heater" — instead of a "call for a quote" page an AI engine has nothing concrete to cite.

  5. 5

    Build a real, distinct page per city or service area you cover — a single generic "we serve the whole region" page gives an AI engine nothing specific to match against a hyper-local query like "plumber in [neighborhood]."

  6. 6

    Surface your licensing, insurance, and certifications in crawlable text, not just a badge image — these are exactly the credibility signals AI engines weigh when deciding which of several similar-looking local businesses to actually name.

  7. 7

    Check your actual AI citation share directly, on a recurring basis — across ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, and Meta AI — rather than assuming a strong Google ranking already covers it. It usually doesn't; that gap is exactly what the 87% and 98.8% figures above describe.

Do homeowners really use ChatGPT to find a plumber or contractor?

Yes. Per Scorpion's 2026 State of Home Services Marketing Report (2,000 homeowners surveyed), 22% of homeowners now go to an AI tool like ChatGPT first, before Google, when they need a contractor. BrightLocal's 2026 Local Consumer Review Survey found a similar shift more broadly: 45% of consumers now use AI tools to find local businesses, up from just 6% a year earlier.

Why don't I show up when someone asks AI for a plumber or HVAC company near them, even though I rank well on Google?

Because Google's local pack and an AI engine's recommendation are two different systems measuring different signals. AI engines weight structured data and cross-platform consistency (Google Business Profile, Angi, Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor, BBB agreeing with each other) and entity strength more heavily than classic local SEO does — which is why a business can rank on page one and still be effectively invisible when someone asks ChatGPT or Gemini directly.
