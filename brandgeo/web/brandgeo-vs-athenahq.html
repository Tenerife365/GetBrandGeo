BrandGEO vs AthenaHQ

Both track how your brand shows up across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and other AI engines, and both turn what they find into concrete next steps — AthenaHQ's Action Center, BrandGEO's "Fix This" hub. Here's an honest look at how their pricing models, engine coverage, and service depth actually differ.

Pricing and features current as of July 2026 — verified directly against AthenaHQ's own pricing page.

Choose BrandGEO if…

You want flat, predictable pricing and a real done-for-you option

You'd rather pay one flat monthly price than watch a credit meter, and you want the option to hand strategy, research, and reporting off to a managed team — without switching tools or hiring a separate agency.

Choose AthenaHQ if…

You want broad enterprise AEO tooling and don't mind usage-based billing

You need citation intelligence, hallucination/discrepancy detection, and BI-tool integrations out of the gate, your usage is predictable enough to plan around a credit allocation, and you're comfortable running the optimization work yourself.

● The Honest Version

Where AthenaHQ genuinely wins

  • Broader engine coverage out of the gate — up to 9 models on Starter, including Google AI Overviews, AI Mode, Copilot, and Grok, versus BrandGEO's 5 core engines.
  • A functional free tier with real monitoring — 300 credits/month, competitor insights, and content recommendations, not just a one-time snapshot.
  • Deeper enterprise-grade tooling — the Athena Citation Engine (ACE), Oracle discrepancy/hallucination detection, SAML/OIDC SSO, and BI integrations (Tableau, Power BI, Looker) on Enterprise.
  • Established market validation — Spring 2026 G2 High Performer and Most Loved Software badges, 4.9/5 rating.

Where BrandGEO genuinely wins

  • Flat, predictable pricing — no credit meter to watch and no overage bills as monitoring frequency or competitor tracking scales up.
  • A real managed-service tier — Managed at €900/month hands the work to a human team; AthenaHQ's Action Center is self-serve software even at Enterprise.
  • Geo-context built into every prompt — responses are gathered as a real local user in-market, not a generic query, plus a maintained Romanian-language sentiment lexicon for non-English brands.
  • A free one-time audit with no signup required, if you just want a baseline before committing to either platform.

Is BrandGEO cheaper than AthenaHQ?

At the entry tier, AthenaHQ's Essential plan is free (300 credits/month) while BrandGEO's paid entry point, Essentials, is €99/month — though BrandGEO also has a free tier plus a one-time no-signup audit. Where AthenaHQ's real cost shows up is usage: its Starter plan is $295/month for 3,600 credits, and extra credits cost roughly $0.08 each, so a busier monitoring program can push the bill well past sticker price. BrandGEO's Growth plan (€299/month) includes 150 prompts across all 5 core engines with no credit meter to manage.

Does AthenaHQ offer a managed service like BrandGEO?

No. AthenaHQ is self-serve software at every published tier, including Enterprise — its Action Center (branded "Content Agents" on their site) and content optimization agents are workflow tools your team runs, not a managed service. Even Enterprise's white-glove setup and enablement is onboarding help, not ongoing execution. BrandGEO offers that same self-serve path on its lower tiers, plus a genuinely managed option on Managed (€900/month), Pro, and Enterprise.

How does AthenaHQ's credit-based pricing compare to BrandGEO's flat pricing?

AthenaHQ bills by credits — 1 credit per AI response, 3,600 included on Starter, with overages priced separately (about $100 per 1,250 extra credits). That model rewards light, disciplined monitoring but gets less predictable as you track more prompts, competitors, or run the Action Center more often. BrandGEO's plans are flat: a set number of prompts across all included engines for one monthly price, so a heavier monitoring cadence doesn't quietly increase your bill.
