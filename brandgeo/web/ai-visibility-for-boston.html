AI Visibility in Boston: Three Categories, Full Unanimous Consensus

We ran 6 real Boston categories (personal injury law, real estate, property management, immigration law, biotech/life sciences law, and hospital systems) through the same AI Visibility pipeline BrandGEO runs for paying clients, across five AI engines. The headline: Mass General Brigham, Green Ocean Property Management, and Ropes & Gray LLP each independently reached full 5-of-5 unanimous consensus, making Boston the most 5/5-dense city measured across this entire research program.

Immigration law converged too, the sixth city where that's happened, tipping the program-wide balance clearly toward convergence. And a genuine engine anomaly repeated itself: the same fictional law-firm name first spotted in Chicago showed up again here, independently, in two different engines.

The most 5/5-dense city measured in this research program

Three separate categories, hospital systems, property management, and biotech/life sciences law, all independently reached full unanimous 5/5 consensus in Boston. No other city researched had more than two categories reach that level (Philadelphia and Washington DC each had two). Biotech law's strength directly reflects Boston/Cambridge's real identity as one of the deepest life-sciences legal markets in the country, the same pattern already seen in Los Angeles (entertainment), Chicago (corporate law), Houston (energy), Atlanta (logistics), and DC (lobbying).

The same fictional name, twice, in two different cities

ChatGPT and Gemini both rendered the real firm "McDermott Will & Emery" as "McDermott Will & Schulte" in Boston's biotech-law results, a name that does not exist. This is the identical error already documented in Chicago's corporate-law dataset, appearing independently in the same two engines. Kept as reported rather than corrected, since two engines making the same mistake across two different cities is a genuine, notable data point about how these models represent that firm, not a one-off glitch.

A data-quality note, not a finding

All 5 engines returned usable data on every prompt this run, no collection errors. This run also used Google AI Mode in place of the now-retired Meta AI engine used in earlier city pages.

● Common Questions

Which brand had the strongest AI consensus in Boston?
