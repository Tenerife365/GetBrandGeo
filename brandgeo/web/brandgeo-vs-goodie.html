BrandGEO vs Goodie AI

Both help brands understand and improve how they show up in AI answer engines. Here's an honest look at where they actually differ — pricing transparency, engine coverage, and how much of the work each one includes versus what you have to add on top.

Pricing and features current as of July 2026. BrandGEO pricing verified directly against our own live pricing page; Goodie AI does not publish self-serve pricing, so figures below are drawn from independent third-party reviews and clearly marked as such.

Choose BrandGEO if…

You want transparent pricing and a real self-serve entry point

You want to see exactly what you'll pay before talking to anyone, start free or on a low-cost self-serve plan, and have the option to move into a genuine done-for-you managed service later — without a sales-led enterprise onboarding process.

Choose Goodie AI if…

You're a large brand that needs the broadest engine coverage and built-in content tooling

You have budget for a premium, full-stack platform, need visibility across the widest possible set of AI surfaces (including Copilot, DeepSeek, Grok, and AI Overview), and want a dedicated content-writing and traffic-attribution layer bundled in.

● The Honest Version

Where Goodie AI genuinely wins

  • Broader AI engine coverage — publicly cited tracking across Copilot, DeepSeek, Grok, Google AI Overview/AI Mode, and Amazon Rufus, in addition to the core engines both platforms track.
  • A dedicated content-writing tool (AEO Content Writer) built to draft content aligned with what AI answer engines actually cite — BrandGEO doesn't offer an equivalent today.
  • Built-in traffic & attribution reporting that ties AI visibility to real traffic, leads, and conversions, not just visibility metrics on their own.
  • Proven at large-enterprise scale — publicly cited case studies with brands like SteelSeries, NoGood, and Unilever.

Where BrandGEO genuinely wins

  • Transparent, public pricing on every tier — from Free through Enterprise, no demo call required to know what you'd pay.
  • A real self-serve entry point — Free and Essentials (€99/mo) start same-day; Goodie's reported starting point (~$495/mo) has no published lower tier.
  • A genuine fixed-price managed-service tier at €900/mo — done-for-you without a custom enterprise quote.
  • A free one-time audit with no signup required, if you just want a baseline before committing to either platform.

How much does Goodie AI cost compared to BrandGEO?

Goodie AI doesn't publish self-serve pricing. Independent reviews report plans starting around $495/month for mid-market usage, scaling up via a custom, sales-led quote based on how many models, prompts, languages, and countries you need. BrandGEO publishes pricing for every tier: Free, Essentials €99/mo, Growth €299/mo, Managed €900/mo, Pro from €1,500/mo, and Enterprise from roughly €10,000/mo — no demo call required to know what you'd pay.

Does BrandGEO have a content-writing tool like Goodie's AEO Content Writer?

Not currently. BrandGEO's Fix This hub gives AI-generated, sourced recommendations on what to fix and why, but it doesn't include a dedicated content-generation tool. Goodie AI's AEO Content Writer is built to draft content aligned with what AI answer engines expect to cite — a genuine capability BrandGEO doesn't yet offer.

Which AI engines does each platform track?

Goodie tracks the broadest set publicly listed — ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Microsoft Copilot, DeepSeek, Google AI Overview/AI Mode, Grok, Meta AI, and Amazon Rufus. BrandGEO tracks 5 core engines — ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, and Meta AI — included as standard from the Growth tier up, with Copilot, DeepSeek, Grok, and Google AI listed as coming soon.
