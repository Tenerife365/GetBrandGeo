AI Visibility in Dublin: When One Engine Answers Every Question the Same Way

BrandGEO ran 8 real commercial-buyer prompts — the kind of question a Dubliner actually types when they need a solicitor, a CRM, or a wealth manager — through its own collection pipeline against four major AI engines, then compared every answer side by side.

Two categories landed on a clean, unanimous 4/4 pick. But the more interesting result is a pattern in how one engine answered: regardless of whether the question was about boutique financial planners or residential property solicitors, it kept reaching for the same set of big, institutional names.

One engine, one answer, regardless of the question

Meta AI's response to "best financial advisors in Dublin" named Davy Private Clients, Investec Wealth & Investment, KBC Wealth, Goodbody Stockbrokers, and Quilter Cheviot — large institutional wealth managers, with zero overlap with the boutique independent planners that Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity all named for the exact same prompt. Ask Meta about wealth management for expats, and the pattern repeats: Davy Wealth, Cantor Fitzgerald, Investec, Brewin Dolphin, Sarasin & Partners — again all large institutions, again with almost no overlap with the other three engines' boutique, expat-focused picks. And for residential property-purchase solicitors, Meta answered with nearly the exact same "Big Five" corporate litigation firms (McCann FitzGerald, A&L Goodbody, Arthur Cox, Mason Hayes & Curran) it had already given for an unrelated commercial-dispute prompt. The firm names Meta extracts are all real — this isn't a data-quality issue. It looks like a genuine tendency to default to well-known, large institutional names regardless of what the query is actually asking for.

The strongest full-consensus rate of any city so far

Two categories — project management software and business banking fintech — landed on a clean 4/4 unanimous top pick, Monday.com and Revolut Business respectively. That's a direct structural contrast to Rome, the previous city in this research program, where zero brands reached even 3-of-4 agreement anywhere in the dataset. Dublin sits at the opposite end of the consensus spectrum.

A data-quality note, not a finding

ChatGPT's collection failed on every Dublin prompt this run (a technical error, `status: error`) — it isn't included in the tables above. Separately, Gemini returned zero structured competitors on 3 of the 8 prompts (employment solicitors, commercial contract dispute, wealth management for expats), despite discussing firms in its own prose for at least two of those — consistent with the same extraction gap already documented in New York (4 of 8) and Rome (5 of 8).

What this means if your business is in Dublin

  1. 1

    Not every engine adapts its answer to your actual query

    If your business competes on being a boutique, independent, or specialist option, don't assume every AI engine will recognize that distinction. At least one major engine in this dataset defaulted to large institutional names regardless of what was being asked.

  2. 2

    A 4/4 unanimous pick is a strong, defensible position

    Monday.com and Revolut Business both hold a rare, fully unanimous top spot across every engine tested. If you're that brand, this is a signal worth protecting; if you're a competitor, it's a signal of exactly how entrenched the incumbent is.

  3. 3

    Check whether an engine can even see your category

    Gemini went silent (zero structured competitors) on more than a third of the Dublin prompts tested, despite discussing real firms in its own text. Confirm the engine actually surfaces your category before assuming poor visibility.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, this week. Extraction quality and engine behavior both shift between runs — a one-time check tells you less than an ongoing one.

● Common Questions

What was the most unusual finding in the Dublin data?

Meta AI showed a recurring big-name bias that ignored what was actually being asked. For financial advisors, wealth management for expats, and property-purchase solicitors, Meta's answers were nearly identical to its answer for an unrelated commercial-litigation prompt — the same large corporate law firms and institutional wealth brands, regardless of query intent. Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity all differentiated cleanly between boutique and institutional options depending on what was asked.

Did any brands achieve full consensus in Dublin?

Yes — two categories reached a clean 4/4 unanimous top pick: Monday.com for project management software, and Revolut Business for business banking fintech. That's the strongest full-consensus rate of any city tested in this research program so far, a direct contrast to Rome, where no brand reached even 3/4 agreement.

Why is ChatGPT missing from this data?

ChatGPT's collection failed on every Dublin prompt this run — a technical error, not evidence ChatGPT has zero visibility for Dublin brands. This page reflects the engines that returned usable results: Claude, Gemini, Meta AI, and Perplexity. A future re-run will include ChatGPT once the collection succeeds.
