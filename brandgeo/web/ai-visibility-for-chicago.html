AI Visibility in Chicago: One Property Manager Sweeps Every Engine

We ran 6 real Chicago categories (personal injury law, real estate, property management, immigration law, corporate law, and commercial real estate) through the same AI Visibility pipeline BrandGEO runs for paying clients, across five AI engines. The headline: Chicago shows the strongest cross-engine consensus of any city researched in this program so far, including the first fully unanimous, 5-of-5, result we've measured.

Property management and corporate law both produced near-total agreement on a single leader. Immigration law, fragmented in every other city tested, broke its own pattern here and converged too. Only individual real estate agents stayed as scattered as ever.

The first full 5/5 consensus measured in this program

Landmark Property Management appears in every single one of the 5 engines' responses for "top-rated property management companies in Chicago", ChatGPT and Claude both rank it #1, Gemini names it #4, Google AI Mode surfaces it by name at 4.8 stars, and Perplexity ranks it #2. New York and Los Angeles both topped out at 4/5 on their strongest categories. This is the cleanest single-brand result measured so far.

Immigration law broke its own pattern

In both New York and Los Angeles, immigration law was one of the most fragmented categories tested, dominated by individually-rated practitioners with almost no cross-engine agreement. In Chicago, Minsky, McCormick & Hallagan, P.C. reached 4 of 5 engines (Claude, Gemini, Google AI Mode, and referenced in Perplexity's prose), and Cipolla Law Group and Aparicio Immigration Law both separately reached 3 of 5. Same methodology, same category type, a genuinely different result, evidence this research reflects real differences between cities rather than a templated write-up.

A data-quality note, not a finding

All 5 engines returned usable data on every prompt this run, no collection errors, matching Los Angeles and improving on New York (where ChatGPT failed on all 8 prompts). One genuine anomaly: for corporate law, both ChatGPT and Gemini independently rendered the real firm "McDermott Will & Emery" as "McDermott Will & Schulte", a name that doesn't exist. Kept as reported rather than silently corrected, since two engines making the identical error independently is itself a notable data point. This run also used Google AI Mode in place of the now-retired Meta AI engine used in earlier city pages.

What this means if your business is in Chicago

  1. 1

    If you compete with a category leader here, the consensus is harder to break than anywhere else measured

    Landmark Property Management and Kirkland & Ellis both show near-total AI agreement. A competitor absent from those answers faces the steepest gap documented in this research program.

  2. 2

    Don't assume your category fragments just because it did in another city

    Immigration law fragmented in New York and Los Angeles but converged in Chicago. Category behavior is genuinely city-specific: check your own market rather than assuming the pattern from elsewhere applies.

  3. 3

    Individual real estate agents are still the easiest door in

    No agent name crossed 2/5 engines, and one engine declined to name anyone at all. That's the category with the most room for a specific agent or team to become the AI-recommended name.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, this week. Engine behavior and extraction quality both shift between runs. A one-time check tells you less than an ongoing one.

● Common Questions

Which brand had the strongest AI consensus in Chicago?

Landmark Property Management, for property management. It's the first fully unanimous result (5 of 5 engines) measured anywhere in this research program, appearing in every single engine's response for that category.

Did any Chicago category buck the usual AI-visibility patterns?

Yes: immigration law. In New York and Los Angeles, immigration law was one of the most fragmented categories tested, dominated by individually-rated practitioners. In Chicago, Minsky, McCormick & Hallagan reached 4 of 5 engine consensus, a genuinely different result under identical methodology.

Do AI engines agree on Chicago's top corporate law firm?

Yes, unanimously. All four AI engines that returned a structured ranking (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) named Kirkland & Ellis as the #1 corporate law firm in Chicago, the clearest single-rank agreement measured in this research program.
