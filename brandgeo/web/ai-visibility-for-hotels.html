AI Visibility for Hotels & Travel Brands: How to Get Recommended by ChatGPT, Gemini & Perplexity

Travelers no longer just search for hotels — increasingly, they ask an AI engine to plan the whole trip and simply tell them where to stay. "Best boutique hotel in Lisbon under $200/night" or "family-friendly resort in Crete with a kids' club" now often gets answered directly, with the traveler never scrolling through an OTA results page or a search-engine list at all. If your property isn't the name the AI engine says out loud, you're invisible at the exact moment the decision gets made.

That's the gap this guide is meant to close.

● What Travelers Actually Ask AI About Hotels

AI engines aren't fielding generic "hotels near me" queries anymore — travelers ask specific, comparison-heavy questions the way they'd ask a well-traveled friend. Here's the shape of what's actually being asked:

Occasion-specific

"What's the best boutique hotel in Lisbon for a honeymoon, under $200 a night?"

Family travel

"I need a family-friendly resort in Crete with a kids' club and a pool for toddlers — what do you recommend?"

Head-to-head comparison

"Is [Hotel A] or [Hotel B] better for a business trip — which has better wifi and a closer walk to the conference center?"

Full itinerary requests

"Plan me a 5-day trip to Porto including where to stay, split between the old town and the coast."

Value/budget framing

"What's the best value hotel in Bali for a couple, with a pool and breakfast included, under $150/night?"

● How to Improve Your AI Visibility as a Hotel or Travel Brand

Most of what drives AI recommendations is the same underlying signal work that drives classic search — but it has to be structured in a way an AI engine can actually parse and trust, not just written for a human skimming a webpage.

1

Add LodgingBusiness / Hotel schema markup

Structured data (schema.org Hotel / LodgingBusiness) gives AI engines hard, parseable facts — star rating, price range, amenities, address — instead of forcing them to infer it from marketing prose.

2

Keep review presence active and current on TripAdvisor, Google, and Booking.com

Review volume, rating, and recency are a documented driver of AI recommendation likelihood — the June 2026 algorithm-audit study above found a top guest rating alone added 31.6 percentage points to a hotel's recommendation probability.

3

Write detailed, parseable amenity and room-type pages

"Family suite, sleeps 4, connecting rooms available, kids' club on-site, shallow pool for toddlers" answers an AI query directly. Vague marketing copy ("unforgettable family memories await") doesn't give the model anything to cite.

4

Earn press and travel-blogger mentions

AI engines lean heavily on independent, third-party sources rather than brand-owned pages alone — being named in a travel blogger's "best boutique hotels in Lisbon" roundup or a press feature carries real weight in what gets cited.

5

Keep your name, address, and phone number (NAP) consistent everywhere

Inconsistent listings across directories, your own site, and OTAs make it harder for an AI engine to confidently resolve which property is actually being discussed — and an unresolved entity doesn't get recommended.

6

Publish direct comparison content

Travelers ask AI engines "X vs Y" questions constantly (see the example prompts above). A page that honestly compares your property against a nearby alternative on the exact criteria travelers ask about gives an AI engine something concrete to cite.

7

Don't assume your AI Visibility is the same in every language

If your guests book in more than one language, check separately — AI training data and source pools skew heavily English, and a property well-cited in English can be effectively invisible the moment a prospect asks in German, Spanish, or Romanian instead. ● Common Questions

Do hotels need a different AI Visibility strategy than other local businesses?

Largely yes. Our broader industry research in BG-008 found AI engines recommend only a small fraction of local businesses despite strong Google Local Pack rankings — but travel queries are also comparison-heavy and detail-specific ("family resort with a kids' club under $250/night"), so hotels need structured, parseable amenity and pricing data on top of the review signals that matter for any local business.

Which AI engines matter most for travel and hospitality searches?

ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity are the three most commonly used for trip planning today, with Claude and Meta AI also increasingly used for itinerary and destination questions. Recommendation behavior varies meaningfully by engine — a hotel that's well-cited on one engine can be completely absent on another, which is why BrandGEO tracks all five engines rather than optimizing for a single one.

How is AI Visibility for hotels different from ranking on TripAdvisor or Google?

TripAdvisor and Google ranking measure whether your listing surfaces in a list a traveler has to browse and compare themselves. AI Visibility measures whether an AI engine names your property directly as the answer to a specific question, often without the traveler ever seeing a list of alternatives at all. A hotel can rank well on both and still never get named by an AI engine, or vice versa.
