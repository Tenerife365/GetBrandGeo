AI Visibility in Washington DC: Two Lobbying Firms Sweep Every Engine

We ran 6 real Washington DC categories (personal injury law, real estate, property management, immigration law, lobbying/government relations, and government contracting) through the same AI Visibility pipeline BrandGEO runs for paying clients, across five AI engines. The headline: Brownstein Hyatt Farber Schreck and BGR both reached full 5-of-5 unanimous presence for lobbying, simultaneously, with 4 of 5 engines each agreeing on their exact rank position too, directly reflecting DC's real identity as the seat of federal lobbying.

Personal injury law and property management both showed solid 3-firm consensus. Immigration law reverted to fragmentation here, evening the program-wide score. Real estate agents and government contracting consultants stayed the most fragmented.

A category that IS the local economy

Brownstein Hyatt Farber Schreck is named by every one of the 5 engines, and 4 of the 5 agree on the exact same rank (#2). BGR is independently named by all 5 engines too, with 4 of 5 agreeing on rank #3. This is the clearest example yet in this research program of a city's strongest AI consensus mapping directly onto its actual economic identity, following the same pattern in Los Angeles (entertainment), Chicago (corporate law), Houston (energy), and Atlanta (logistics).

Immigration law evens the program-wide score

Chicago, Miami, Philadelphia, Atlanta, and Phoenix all showed immigration law converging into real consensus, a five-city streak. Washington DC broke it: no firm reached 3/5, with only Haynes Novick Kohn showing loose 2/5 overlap. That makes DC the fifth fragmented city (joining New York, Los Angeles, Houston, and Dallas), evening the program-wide tally at exactly 5 vs. 5.

A data-quality note, not a finding

All 5 engines returned usable data on every prompt this run, no collection errors. Four legal-directory platforms were misidentified as law firms in a single response and removed as noise: "Avvo," "Martindale-Hubbell," "Super Lawyers," and "Best Lawyers." This run also used Google AI Mode in place of the now-retired Meta AI engine used in earlier city pages.

What this means if your business is in Washington DC

  1. 1

    If you're in lobbying or government relations, the incumbent bar is the highest measured

    Brownstein Hyatt Farber Schreck and BGR both show near-total AI agreement, on rank, not just presence. A competitor absent from those answers faces the steepest documented gap in this category type anywhere in this research.

  2. 2

    Personal injury law and property management are winnable, not locked down

    Both categories show three separate firms at the same 3/5 consensus level, no single entrenched leader. There's real room to enter the consensus set without unseating a dominant incumbent.

  3. 3

    Real estate agents and government contracting consultants are the easiest doors in

    Neither category has a name crossing 2/5. That's the best-documented opportunity in DC for a specific agent or consulting firm to become the AI-recommended name.

  4. 4

    Re-run this regularly, not once

    These answers reflect what the engines say today, this week. Engine behavior and extraction quality both shift between runs. A one-time check tells you less than an ongoing one.
