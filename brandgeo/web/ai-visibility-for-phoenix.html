AI Visibility in Phoenix: Consensus Becomes the Norm

We ran 6 real Phoenix categories (personal injury law, real estate, property management, immigration law, retirement/senior living, and home services) through the same AI Visibility pipeline BrandGEO runs for paying clients, across five AI engines. The headline: Phoenix became the fifth city in this research program where immigration law converged instead of fragmenting, meaning convergent cities now outnumber fragmented ones program-wide for the first time.

Personal injury law showed a clean 4-of-5 leader, and property management showed an unusually wide spread of consensus, five separate firms all tied at 3/5, rather than one dominant name. Home services and individual real estate agents stayed the most fragmented, as usual.

Immigration-law convergence is now the majority pattern

Habich Law PLC was named the top pick by ChatGPT's prose, Claude's structured list, and Gemini's prose, 3 of 5 engines. That makes Phoenix the fifth city (after Chicago, Miami, Philadelphia, and Atlanta) where immigration law converged instead of fragmenting, tipping the program-wide balance: 5 convergent cities now outnumber 4 fragmented ones (New York, Los Angeles, Houston, Dallas).

Property management showed a wide spread, not one leader

Unlike Chicago (one firm at 5/5) or Houston and Philadelphia (firms at 4/5), Phoenix's property-management category showed five separate firms, Real Property Management Evolve, Stratton Vantage Property Management, On Q Property Management, AZ Prime Property Management, and Service Star Realty, all independently reaching exactly 3/5. The category type remains reliably high-consensus across every city researched, just distributed differently here.

A data-quality note, not a finding

All 5 engines returned usable data on every prompt this run, no collection errors. Three directory/matching platforms were misidentified as real estate agents and removed as noise: "Zillow," "Agent Pronto," and "Yelp." This run also used Google AI Mode in place of the now-retired Meta AI engine used in earlier city pages.
