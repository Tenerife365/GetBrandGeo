AI Visibility & Generative Engine Optimization

AI Visibility is a brand's ability to be mentioned, recommended, or cited in answers generated by AI engines — including ChatGPT (OpenAI), Gemini (Google), Claude (Anthropic), Perplexity AI, and Meta AI (Meta).

It is entirely distinct from traditional search ranking. A brand can be on page one of Google and still be completely absent from every AI engine's recommendations — because AI engines do not index the web the same way search engines do. They synthesize, weigh, and generate answers based on a different set of signals.

AI Visibility is measured by how frequently, how positively, and in how many relevant contexts an AI engine mentions your brand when responding to queries your potential customers would actually ask.

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing your brand's online presence so that AI engines are more likely to mention, recommend, or cite you in their generated answers.

GEO focuses on three core pillars:

  • Entity recognition — ensuring AI engines know your brand exists as a distinct, credible entity
  • Concept association — ensuring your brand is connected to the right categories, problems, and use cases in AI knowledge bases
  • Trust signals — building the third-party references, authoritative content, and structured data that AI engines weight as reliability indicators

GEO is different from SEO in that it does not optimize for keyword rankings or click-through rates — it optimizes for being cited inside AI-generated answers that users receive without ever clicking a link.

SEO and AI Visibility are two completely separate visibility dimensions:

  • SEO gets you traffic — users click a search result and visit your website
  • AI Visibility gets you citations — your brand is named inside an AI-generated answer, often without any link being clicked

A brand that ranks first on Google for "best catering company in Madrid" may still be completely absent when a user asks ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity the same question. The two channels require different content strategies, different authority signals, and different measurement frameworks.

As AI search continues to grow, brands that only optimize for traditional SEO are becoming increasingly invisible to a significant — and growing — share of their potential customers.

AI-powered search is growing at approximately 4× year-over-year. Platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini are rapidly becoming the first stop for consumers seeking recommendations, comparisons, and buying guidance.

The critical window is now: citation patterns in AI engines are still being established. Brands that invest in GEO early build a structural advantage that compounds over time — similar to early SEO movers in the 2000s. This advantage will become increasingly difficult to replicate as AI citation patterns solidify.

Less than 10% of brands currently monitor or optimize their AI Visibility. The gap between early movers and the rest is growing every week.

The AI Visibility Score

The AI Visibility Score is a weighted composite across six dimensions, measured for your brand across all five AI engines each week:

  • Recognition — Does the engine know your brand exists?
  • Knowledge — Does it have accurate, detailed information about you?
  • Sentiment — Is the language used positive, neutral, or negative?
  • Accuracy — Are the facts it states about you correct?
  • Reach — How many relevant query types surface your brand?
  • Consistency — Does the engine give consistent answers across different queries?

Each dimension is scored per engine and combined into the overall 0–100 score. The score is recalculated every week so you can track your trend over time.

Each AI engine is assigned a status per query category based on how it responded:

  • KNOW — The engine mentioned your brand accurately and confidently. This is the target state.
  • PARTIAL — The engine mentioned your brand but with uncertainty, incomplete information, or mixed in with incorrect details.
  • MISSING — The engine did not mention your brand at all, or named a competitor instead.

The Fix This action hub prioritises the most impactful steps to move engines from MISSING → PARTIAL → KNOW.

When an AI engine mentions multiple brands in a single answer, BrandGEO records the position at which your brand was mentioned. Being mentioned first (#1) carries the highest weight in the score calculation, since AI engine users tend to act on whichever brand is named first.

Improving your position from #3 to #1 within an engine that already knows you is often faster and higher-value than trying to appear in engines that don't mention you at all.
