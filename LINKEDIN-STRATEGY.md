# LinkedIn Visibility Plan — 2026-07-15

Constraint: no ad budget right now. Existing asset: a posting plan
(`linkedin-posts-YYYY-MM-DD.md`, auto-generated weekly per CLAUDE.md §9.23).
Goal: maximize organic reach using channels already available for free,
before any ad spend exists.

## 1. On free ad credits — checked live, verdict: don't rely on this

Researched current LinkedIn advertiser promotions rather than assuming.
Two real offers exist, neither solves the "zero budget" problem:

- **LinkedIn's standard new-advertiser offer is "spend $250, get $250
  free"** — a 2x match, not free money. It requires committing $250 of
  real spend first. Not usable until there's a budget to begin with.
- **Small Business Digital Alliance's $100 in free LinkedIn ad credits**
  looks like a genuine no-spend-required grant (sign up via their LinkedIn
  offer page), but the exact current terms weren't independently
  confirmable beyond their own listing page. Worth 5 minutes to check and
  claim if it really is no-strings — one action item below — but don't
  build the plan around it. $100 covers a few days of a small campaign at
  best.

Bottom line: there is no meaningful free ad tier for a company with zero
prior spend. The real lever right now is organic, native-feature reach,
which costs nothing and is under-used.

## 2. The one structural fact that should shape everything else

BrandGEO is a solo-founder company (autónomo, 1 person). There is no
employee network to amplify posts the way a 20-person company would.
**Constantin's personal profile is the highest-leverage channel, not the
Company Page** — LinkedIn's algorithm gives personal profiles far more
organic reach than Company Pages by default. The Company Page still
matters (legitimacy, link target, schema/Crunchbase consistency) but
should be treated as secondary distribution, not primary.

## 3. Two native features to add, both free, both a strong content fit

From the LinkedIn "Create" menu:

- **Newsletter.** Turn the monthly AI Visibility Index report into an
  actual LinkedIn Newsletter, not just a link post. A newsletter
  auto-notifies every subscriber the moment a new issue publishes — this
  is free, built-in, recurring distribution for content already being
  produced monthly (Issue #1 already exists at
  `/ai-visibility-index-2026-07.html`). This is the single highest-value
  addition here: it turns a monthly report into a compounding subscriber
  list instead of a one-off post.
- **Publish an Article.** LinkedIn's native long-form articles get
  indexed and surfaced differently than a link-out post — a link post
  gets throttled by LinkedIn's own algorithm (it prefers to keep people
  on-platform), a native article does not. Republish the strongest 2-3
  existing BG-articles as native LinkedIn Articles (adapted, not pasted
  verbatim — same facts, written for the platform), then do this for
  every new BG-article going forward as a standing step alongside
  publishing it on the site.

Not recommended yet: **Showcase Page** (only useful once the Company Page
itself has real followers to sub-segment; premature at this stage) and
**Create an Ad** (needs budget, out of scope per the constraint above).

## 4. Sequenced steps

1. **This week — claim the SBDA $100 credit** (5 min, no downside, keep
   in reserve rather than banking on it).
2. **This week — rewrite Constantin's personal LinkedIn profile** around
   BrandGEO specifically: headline states the AI-visibility positioning
   directly, About section links `getbrandgeo.com`, Featured section
   pins the Index report + top 2 BG-articles, banner image matches site
   branding.
3. **This week — publish the AI Visibility Index Issue #1 as a LinkedIn
   Newsletter's first edition** (not just a regular post about it). This
   is the one irreversible-compounding move: every future issue then
   reaches accumulated subscribers automatically.
4. **This week — publish 2 native LinkedIn Articles** from the strongest
   existing BG-content (BG-016 cross-engine consensus and one city-
   research page are strong candidates — original data, not opinion).
   Going forward, one native article per new BG-article, standing
   practice.
5. **Ongoing — keep the existing weekly-generated posting cadence**
   (`linkedin-posts-*.md`, already split Personal vs. Company Page per
   §9.23), posted from the personal profile as primary, Company Page as
   secondary/lighter repost.
6. **Ongoing — a daily 15-minute engagement block.** Comment genuinely
   (not generic "Great post!") on 5-10 posts from larger accounts in the
   AI-search/GEO/marketing space. With no employee network to amplify
   posts, real engagement on other people's content is the substitute
   distribution mechanism, and it's free.
7. **Weekly — check LinkedIn as a referral source in GA4** (GA4 is
   already wired sitewide per §7.10/§7.12). Track referral sessions from
   linkedin.com, not just LinkedIn's own vanity impression counts, so the
   real signal (site traffic that converts) is what's being optimized
   for, not just reach.

Use LinkedIn's standard "spend $250, get $250 free" new-advertiser match
