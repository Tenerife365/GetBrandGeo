### Draft copy

**We asked four AI assistants to recommend a London law firm. In one practice area, they couldn't agree on a single name.**

We ran eight commercial questions — the kind a client actually types — through four AI assistants (Claude, Gemini, Meta AI, Perplexity), and recorded every firm each one named.

Some categories have a clear default:

→ **Commercial contract dispute** — Clifford Chance was named by every engine that answered.
→ **Property purchase** — Farrer & Co was named by three of the four.

Employment law was different.

Three engines answered. They returned almost entirely different firms — barely one name in common between them. One leaned toward large City practices. Another surfaced claimant and union-side firms. There was no shared answer.

Here's why that matters.

**When AI assistants converge on a name, that name is hard to displace.** It has quietly become the default answer — and it will be repeated to every client who asks.

**When they don't converge, the category is still unclaimed.** Nobody owns the AI narrative yet. The firms that move first get to become the default.

Right now, employment law in London is wide open.

Most firms have no idea whether an AI assistant recommends them, ignores them, or names a competitor instead. It isn't in your analytics. It doesn't show up in your rankings. And by the time it does, the default is set.

We measure it — and then we do the work to fix it. Not a dashboard for you to interpret. A service we run for you.

**The full London research — all 8 questions, every firm named, broken down by engine:** https://getbrandgeo.com/ai-visibility-for-london.html *(confirmed live)*
