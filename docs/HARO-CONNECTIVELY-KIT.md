# BrandGEO — Journalist Source Outreach Kit (Connectively, formerly HARO)

A ready-to-use kit for getting BrandGEO/Constantin quoted in real journalism
and industry publications — genuine expert commentary in exchange for real
editorial coverage, the legitimate practice kept from the
`BrandGEO_OffSite_Growth_Blueprint.pdf` review (the manipulative parts —
link-injection network, mass resource-page pitching — were declined per
`rules/content-integrity.md`).

Note: "HARO" (Help a Reporter Out) was rebranded to **Connectively** by
Cision in 2025 — same service, same journalist-source-matching model, new
name. If you land on a page still called HARO, that's the same product.

## Step 1 — Create the source account (~5 minutes, your action)

1. Go to [connectively.us](https://connectively.us) (or search
   "Connectively HARO source signup" if that URL has changed by the time
   you read this).
2. Sign up as a **Source** (not a journalist) — free tier exists, paid
   tiers exist for higher query volume/AI-assisted matching, free tier is
   enough to start.
3. Use `support@getbrandgeo.com` or your own email — whichever you want
   journalist replies going to.
4. When asked for topics/categories to receive queries for, select:
   - Business & Finance
   - Technology
   - High Tech
   - General (some platforms bucket marketing/SEO queries here)
   - If a dedicated "Marketing" or "SEO/Search" category exists, select
     that too — this is exactly BrandGEO's wheelhouse and the highest-
     signal category to watch.

## Step 3 — What topics to actually pitch on

Only respond to queries that are a genuine fit — a scattershot approach
gets a source flagged as spam, and a real quote requires real expertise,
not a forced angle. Genuine-fit query topics for BrandGEO, roughly ranked
by how directly they map to existing, sourced expertise:

1. **"How is AI changing search/SEO?"** — directly BrandGEO's core subject.
2. **"What is GEO / Generative Engine Optimization?"** — BG-001 already
   defines this in depth; can quote/paraphrase from that published article.
3. **"How do I know if my brand shows up in ChatGPT/AI answers?"** — direct
   product fit.
4. **"AI and small business marketing"** — BrandGEO's ICP overlaps here.
5. **"2026 marketing trends"** — general trend-roundup queries, where a
   GEO/AI-visibility angle is a genuinely fresh addition most other sources
   won't offer.
6. **"Multilingual / international marketing"** — BG-014 and the Berlin/
   Madrid/Paris/Rome bilingual research give a real, sourced angle here
   most sources can't speak to.

## Step 4 — Ready talking points (sourced from BrandGEO's own published research)

Pull from these rather than inventing new stats on the fly — every number
below is already published, verified, and sourced on getbrandgeo.com, so a
journalist fact-checking the quote will find it holds up:

- **93%** of Google AI Mode queries now end in zero clicks to any website
  (Semrush, cited in BG-005) — the single strongest "why this matters"
  stat for a general audience.
- **48%** of tracked Google queries now show an AI Overview, up from 31% a
  year earlier (BrightEdge, cited in BG-005).
- **75–85%** of Perplexity's commercial-query answers cite a named brand —
  the highest citation rate of any engine tested (XLR8 AI 200-query study,
  cited in BG-006).
- Brands mentioned by AI engines see roughly **35% higher organic
  click-through rate** than unmentioned brands (Averi/Wellows research,
  cited in BG-012).
- In BrandGEO's own city-by-city research program, several categories
  showed **no cross-engine consensus at all** — e.g. Rome, where zero
  brands achieved agreement across 3+ AI engines for the same query — a
  concrete, real example of how fragmented and unpredictable AI visibility
  currently is (see `ai-visibility-for-rome.html`).
- Also from BrandGEO's own research: language changes not just which
  brand gets recommended but sometimes the *entire category* considered —
  e.g. in Paris, a French-language wealth-management query surfaced
  boutique French firms, while the identical query in English surfaced
  major international private banks (see `ai-visibility-for-paris.html`).

## Step 5 — Pitch email template

Fill in the bracketed parts per query; keep the reply itself tight —
journalists skim dozens of pitches per query, 100–150 words including the
quote is the sweet spot.

```
Subject: Re: [exact query subject line from the alert email]

Hi [journalist name if given, else "there"],

[1 sentence establishing direct relevance — why you're a fit for this specific query, not a generic intro.]

"[Your actual quote — 2-4 sentences, specific and quotable, not a vague platitude. Lead with a concrete stat or observation, not 'I think that...']"

— Constantin Daniel, founder of BrandGEO (getbrandgeo.com), an AI Visibility Intelligence platform

Happy to expand on any of this or provide additional data if useful — [phone/email] works best for a quick follow-up if you're on deadline.
```

## Step 6 — Monitoring cadence

Connectively/HARO sends query digest emails multiple times per day. Realistic
cadence given this isn't a full-time job:
- Check the digest once a day (mornings tend to have the freshest queries —
  older ones often already have enough responses).
- Respond within a few hours of a query landing if it's a strong fit —
  journalists often close out queries once they have enough good
  responses, so speed matters more than polish.
- Skip anything requiring niche expertise BrandGEO doesn't actually have —
  a bad-fit quote that gets published looks worse than no quote at all.

## What NOT to do (carried over from the declined blueprint review)

- Don't submit the same quote to unrelated queries just to get published —
  every pitch should be genuinely written for that specific question.
- Don't misrepresent BrandGEO's size, funding, or client roster in a pitch
  to sound more authoritative than it is.
- Don't treat this as a link-building tactic first and expert commentary
  second — the moment a source starts optimizing for the backlink instead
  of giving a real answer, it stops being legitimate PR and starts being
  exactly the kind of scheme this project has repeatedly declined to run.
