## This week's focus: BG-018, a methodology piece on measurement accuracy

**Topic:** how BrandGEO cleaned up false-positive competitor extraction and
false-negative brand mentions in its own AI-response analysis pipeline. This
draws on real, already-shipped, already-verified engineering work: the
`_analysis.js` accuracy audit (CLAUDE.md section 8, rounds 1 through 5 of
competitor-extraction fixes, sentiment scoped to the brand's own clause,
mention-detection word boundaries, position-unit correction, the Claude
stream-truncation fix) and the newer semantic competitor gate (an LLM
classifier layered on top of the structural filters, section 8.16). Every
claim in this angle is backed by code that is committed and, per CLAUDE.md,
already validated against live collection data (see section 8.15's
2026-07-15/16 live verification and section 8.16's 14-assertion mocked test).

**Why this and not something else:** BG-018 is next in the numbered research
series (BG-001 through BG-017 are published, most recently BG-017 on
2026-07-16, four days ago). It continues the established weekly cadence
without inventing a new initiative type. It needs no new prompts fired at
any AI engine, so there is nothing for you to click before Wednesday. And
unlike a couple of other candidates considered below, every fact in it is
already true and already shipped, so there is no risk of the article getting
ahead of the product.

**Suggested angles for Wednesday to choose between (not a full outline,
that is Wednesday's job):**
1. A narrative walkthrough of the five extraction-fix rounds, with the real
   before/after examples already documented in CLAUDE.md section 8 (bold
   section headings, colon-terminated field labels, numbered evaluation
   criteria, the emoji-prefixed rank bug), ending on the shift to a semantic
   LLM gate instead of an ever-growing denylist.
2. A narrower, more citable piece specifically on why keyword-based

   whether the recommendation badge sat inside or outside the heading).

Either is legitimate, verifiable content, not manufactured urgency.
